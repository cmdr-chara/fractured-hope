# Prologue

The prologue of _Fractured Hope_ — told across _Fractured Beginnings_ (0.1) and _Echoes of Reset_ (0.2) — establishes the present-day state of [[characters/chara|Chara]], the world she inhabits, and the rupture that sets the larger story in motion.

## Summary

At twenty-five, Chara lives in a city she half-built herself. Her apartment is functional rather than comfortable. The locket around her neck holds a photograph of [[characters/raiden|Raiden]]. After [[lore/lumina|Lumina fragmentation]], the photograph is replaced by her own reflection, though the locket stays.

The prologue establishes the core tension without resolving it: Chara possesses [[lore/determination|Determination]] powerful enough to have survived [[lore/lab-c7|Lab C7]], a six-month government detention when she was sixteen, and everything that followed — yet that same power has been documented, measured, and weaponized against her by the institutions that should have protected her. The extinction probability codename surfaces only as a number in a file Chara has already memorized.

[[characters/frisk|Frisk]] appears in these pages with eyes the manuscript describes as "once turquoise, now darker and sharp." She carries the wasp knife on Raiden's instructions. Their first exchange in the present day is careful rather than warm. The weight of her complicity in the Library and Lab C7 is not surfaced yet, but the distance between them signals it.

The attack itself is not random. Raiden's [[lore/lumina|Lumina]] is deliberately fragmented and scattered across realities. Everyone who knew him begins losing their memories at a rate proportional to connection depth. For Chara, the loss is near-total.

_Echoes of Reset_ (0.2) follows Chara's decision to use the reset contingency Raiden had prepared. The reset is not a triumph. It is temporal mutilation: she goes backward carrying grief, rage, and fractured memory. She does not come back whole.

## Raiden's Failsafe

The prologue establishes several artifacts Raiden had prepared in advance of catastrophe:

- A holographic message device, hidden beneath a fixed-point building that resists reality instability
- A note tucked under a flower — lily imagery recurs here and in Arc 4.6
- Soul-stabilization notes with precise emotional and physical anchoring data for Chara
- The research that will eventually become the full soul fusion plan

These artifacts are not all found in the Prologue itself. Some are recovered across Arcs 1 and 2. The Prologue introduces the architecture of the failsafe; later arcs reveal its contents.

## Narrative Function

The prologue withholds more than it reveals. The reset anchor exists, but its cost is not yet stated. [[characters/kager|Kager]] is referenced obliquely. The story is told close to Chara's perspective, in a voice that is precise about physical detail and guarded about emotional inference.

Its primary work is register. The manuscript is dark, careful, and specific. The prologue makes that clear before the first main arc begins.

## Unresolved at This Point

> **Unresolved:** The prologue does not specify what event directly triggers Chara's re-entry into conflict. The manuscript implies a threshold was crossed, but the mechanism is not named until Arc 1.

> **Unresolved:** The identity of the second actor in Raiden's Lumina fragmentation is not stated in the Prologue. It surfaces as a thread in Arc 3 and remains open in reviewed sources.
