# Arc 1: A New Dawn

Arc 1 is the opening arc of the main story, picking up after [[characters/chara|Chara]]'s use of [[characters/raiden|Raiden]]'s reset contingency at the end of the Prologue. She has gone backward, but the reset has not restored her. She returns with grief, fractured memory, and no clean starting point.

## Major Events

### 1.1 — Return and Reconnection

Chara comes back to an earlier state of the world and makes contact with [[characters/frisk|Frisk]]. Their relationship is not warm. It is historically loaded: Frisk knows things about Chara's past that Chara cannot yet fully remember, and the guilt of the Library and [[lore/lab-c7|Lab C7]] sits unspoken between them.

The chapter establishes Liberado's state in this timeline and Chara's position inside it: commander, symbol, and person, all three categories under stress simultaneously.

Chara cannot fully reconstruct who she lost. She knows Raiden is important. She cannot recover all of why.

### 1.2 — Violence as Coping

Chara kills enemies and feels a brief high, then sinks into hollowness. The arc does not present this as simple catharsis. It is a coping mechanism that doesn't resolve anything.

Flashbacks begin to surface — fragments of torture, containment, and dehumanization. The manuscript does not insert these cleanly. They arrive as intrusions.

### 1.3 — Fragmented Memory and Soul Metaphysics

The arc deepens its handling of what [[lore/lumina|Lumina]] fragmentation actually does to memory. Emotional resonance survives longer than factual recall. Chara still feels the weight of who she lost before she can fully reconstruct his name or face.

Frisk is also shown to carry broken memories of Raiden. The fragmentation is not exclusive to Chara; it scales with connection depth.

### 1.4 — The Device and the Names

Raiden's damaged holographic device surfaces. The message is partial and damaged, but two names come through clearly: **Kager** and **[[lore/jikle|Jikle]]**.

This is the turn. The arc shifts from grief-as-collapse into grief-as-investigation. Chara now has something to move toward. She also has [[characters/axel|Axel]] — Raiden's covert collaborator — who makes contact with the information that he retained memory of Raiden across resets using synthesized Determination micro-doses.

Axel knows more than he has explained. This arc establishes that without resolving it.

## Lore Revelations

- **Lumina fragmentation causes memory loss proportional to connection strength.** This is confirmed in the text as a mechanic, not a metaphor.
- **Axel used synthesized Determination** to retain core memories across timeline resets. This is the first appearance of the technique and the first suggestion that Determination can be extracted, synthesized, and dosed externally.
- **The reset did not restore Chara.** She came back carrying the damage.

## Character Movement

- **Chara** — returns post-reset, fights reflexively, begins to piece together what she lost and why it matters
- **Frisk** — present and burdened; her complicity is not surfaced here, but the distance between them signals something
- **Axel** — appears as the keeper of Raiden's deeper project; dangerous trust offered at an early moment
- **Raiden** — present only through the device and through what Chara cannot remember

## Thematic Significance

Arc 1 is the story refusing to let a reset function as a clean slate. Every version of the story in which reset-logic offers relief gets blocked here. The violence does not help. The return does not heal. What it does is establish a direction.

## Unresolved
