# Chara and Raiden

[[characters/raiden|Raiden]] is described in the manuscript as Chara's "boyfriend, vice president, reason." Of the three, the third carries the most structural weight. His presence organizes her relationship to Liberado, to the possibility of rest, and to the question the story keeps returning to: whether love is a solution or a complication.

[[characters/kager|Kager]] frames the answer as the latter. The manuscript answers differently.

## The First Embrace

The clearest single scene documenting this relationship appears in Arc 4.6, rendered as a flashback set at a beach. The manuscript describes it this way:

> _"He hugged me. Just like that. Strong arms, yet his movements were strangely gentle — like handling something precious that had been broken too many times."_

A knife drops from Chara's hand during this moment. The manuscript does not editorialize. The image carries the argument.

## The Locket

Chara wears a heart-shaped locket holding Raiden's photograph. After [[lore/lumina|Lumina]] fragmentation, the photograph is gone. The locket shows only her own reflection. She keeps wearing it.

## The Reset Anchor

Before his death, Raiden built the reset anchor — described as "a dim yellow light" — and gave it to Chara. The manuscript calls it his "final gift," designed for "the moment when she would be alone and broken."

The anchor is severed permanently during Arc 2.3 as a consequence of soul fusion. After that point, no resets remain available.

## The Wasp Knife

Raiden gave the wasp knife to [[characters/frisk|Frisk]] years before the main story, with instructions: _"If I'm not there to save her, give her this. It's the only thing that can remind her who she is."_ Frisk carries it and delivers it to Chara. The knife is a message rather than a weapon.

## His Final Message

Embedded in the wasp knife during Arc 5:

> _"If you're hearing this, you're about to become me. Don't. Choose the pain. Choose the failure. Choose them."_

This is the clearest statement of the manuscript's central argument. It is addressed to a specific person at a specific moment of collapse, and it asks her not to optimize the loss away.

## Kager's Framing

Kager describes the origin of his and Raiden's shared project:

> _"Raiden and I built the theory together. Perfect universe, perfect results. No more variables, no more loss. But he got distracted. He found you. Started talking about love as if it was a solution instead of a complication... love is the system itself. And systems that run on love are designed to fail."_

The manuscript treats this as a real position, not a cartoon one. Raiden left it anyway.

## After Arc 5

The amber-light resolution of Arc 5 is rendered in the manuscript as:

> _"The crystalline memory didn't shatter. It blossomed. Into a thousand tiny moments of imperfect love."_

And:

> _"Some devastations are worth choosing."_

Raiden does not return. The manuscript does not soften this. What survives is the choice he asked Chara to make, which she makes.

## Unresolved

> **Unresolved:** The exact circumstances of Raiden's death are not fully detailed in the manuscript. The relationship between his genocide route book and [[lore/lab-c7|Lab C7]] is also not explained.
