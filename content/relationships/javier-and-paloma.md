# Javier and Paloma

[[characters/javier|Javier]] and [[characters/paloma|Paloma]] enter _Fractured Hope_ as agents of [[characters/kager|Kager]]'s orbit and leave it as something the manuscript has carefully, deliberately made real before it takes away. Their relationship is one of the story's central mirrors: love, loyalty, future-planning, and the specific power that emerges when bonds are literal rather than metaphorical.

## How They Enter

At the Arc 3 concert, Javier is the opponent. Paloma is the reason [[characters/chara|Chara]] does not kill him.

Paloma throws herself between Chara and Javier after the fight, offering herself as a shield and trading information for their lives. The reason she acts is not strategic — it is protective in the most direct sense. Javier matters to her more than survival calculus.

Chara does not kill him. She hears them talk afterward. The future they describe — quiet, coastal, a bakery — is too small and too specific to dismiss. She lets them go.

## The Bakery Dream

Late in the story, Javier and Paloma talk about what they want after the war: a bakery somewhere near the coast. The conversation is soft and practical, with the specific quality of something that has been discussed before and kept returning.

The manuscript gives them this dream precisely so that it can be taken away. It is not filler. It is the future that won't exist, placed in the story early enough that the reader carries it into Arc 5.

## Javier's Confession

During the Arc 3 fight, Javier confirms his role in [[characters/raiden|Raiden]]'s Lumina fragmentation:

> _"I lent a hand. As did someone else you know."_

He does not name the second person. The confession is not framed as pride; it is delivered as acknowledgment. He did a thing and it contributed to a specific, targeted destruction. That fact coexists with his relationship with Paloma and the bakery dream and his later place in the household. The manuscript does not clean this up.

## The Household

After Arc 3, Javier and Paloma join the household. The domestic period of Arc 4 includes them in the ordinary daily texture: meals, proximity, small jokes, the kind of presence that makes a group feel like people rather than a unit.

Their absorption into the household is one of the story's most important structural moves. It means Kager's later action against them registers as the destruction of a home rather than the elimination of opponents.

## Javier's Death

[[characters/kager|Kager]] kills Javier in Arc 5.3. The method is brutal and deliberate: he weaponizes a severed limb into a blade and cuts Javier in half at the waist. The scene is not incidental. Kager is demonstrating what his worldview does to people like Javier: people who were allowed to become real.

[[characters/frisk|Frisk]] saves Javier's soul and Lumina before Kager can destroy them. The Lumina ends up in a glass container on Chara's desk.

## Paloma After

Javier's death transforms Paloma. Her bond-powered ability scales with emotional connection and burns life-force per use. After his death, the source material confirms she attacks Kager with star-level force — something close to cosmic artillery.

She does not kill him. She does strain him, which is one of the only times in the manuscript that Kager appears genuinely pressed.

She escapes afterward. In Arc 6.2, Frisk reports that Paloma finished the revolver — the one Sans delivers to Chara before the final fight.

## Their Function in the Story

Javier and Paloma mirror [[relationships/chara-and-raiden|Chara and Raiden]] in a specific way: love, loyalty, and a future worth fighting for. The manuscript uses this parallel deliberately. When Kager destroys Javier, he is not just killing a character who has earned the reader's attention. He is demonstrating what his worldview does to the very thing Raiden chose over Kager's project.

> **Confirmed:** Their bond-power dynamic — Paloma's strength scaling with their connection — is explicitly described in the source material.

## Open Questions

> **Unresolved:** Paloma's post-Arc 6 location and status are not detailed beyond the revolver delivery.
