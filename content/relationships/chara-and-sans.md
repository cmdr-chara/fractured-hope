# Chara and Sans

[[characters/chara|Chara]] and [[characters/sans|Sans]] have one of the stranger relationships in _Fractured Hope_, built across opposite ends of the same history. He helped imprison her. He promised her person he loved most to protect her. He makes pie after her sister dies and cannot say the name.

None of that is resolved. It just accumulates.

## The Library and the Judgment Hall

In the backstory confirmed across multiple source sections:

- After Chara escaped [[lore/lab-c7|Lab C7]] at sixteen, she was taken to [[locations/the-library|the Grand Library]]
- Sans manifested the **Judgment Hall** inside it and **crushed her soul**
- [[characters/frisk|Frisk]] locked the door

Sans was part of the institutional machinery that decided Chara needed to be contained after she survived Lab C7. The extinction probability framing — 99.4%, codename THE RED GENOCIDE — existed when he helped imprison her. Whether he read the file is not confirmed. Whether he would have acted differently if he had is also not confirmed.

This is the foundation of their present relationship: he has already done the worst thing he was willing to do, and she is still alive, and they both know it.

## Raiden's Request

By Arc 4.1, Sans reveals he had an arrangement with [[characters/raiden|Raiden]]. Raiden asked him to watch over Chara if something happened — not to interfere, not to control, just to be present and to hold the line if everything was about to collapse.

The instruction:

> _"Don't interfere unless she's about to bring the whole damn house down."_

Sans has been keeping that promise for eight years. He keeps it across the main narrative: present, watchful, quiet, and not in her way unless he has to be.

## Present-Day Care

The manuscript documents several of the story's smallest acts of sustained attention through Sans:

- cooking for the household in the middle arcs
- checking on Chara when soul fusion takes a physical toll
- noticing security weaknesses around the house
- maintaining normalcy where the situation allows it

None of these are announced. The story places them and moves on.

## Arc 6.1 — The Pie Scene

After Frisk's death, Sans is in the kitchen making pie. He cannot say her name directly. Chara learns what happened through soul-spike before he speaks.

The scene does not stage grief dramatically. Sans keeps doing the task. The pie is not a resolution or a symbol he has chosen; it is just the thing his hands are doing. The manuscript lets that be enough.

## Arc 6.2 — The Hug

Before Chara leaves for the Arc 6 fight, Sans delivers the revolver [[characters/paloma|Paloma]] built and hugs her. The hug is brief.

The source material frames this as the quiet conclusion of the eight-year promise: _"Don't interfere unless she's about to bring the whole damn house down."_ He has not interfered. The hug is what eight years of watching, and restraining himself, costs.

## The Shape of the Relationship

The manuscript does not frame this relationship as repaired or healed. There is no scene where Sans apologizes for the Judgment Hall or where Chara forgives him. What exists is more uncomfortable and more honest: they are in each other's lives because Raiden asked, because they both love Frisk, because the house needs someone to cook in it, and because at some point in the last eight years they stopped being former captor and former prisoner and became something harder to name.

## Thematic Function

Sans represents the cost of learning too late. He helped build a cage and then had to live long enough to understand who was inside it. His relationship with Chara is the story's clearest example of what it looks like to remain in relationship with someone you have genuinely harmed without the comfort of resolution.
