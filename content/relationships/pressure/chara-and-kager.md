# Chara and Kager

[[characters/chara|Chara]] and [[characters/kager|Kager]] are the story's clearest ideological opponents. Kager treats love as a flaw to map and weaponize. Chara keeps choosing people even when those choices cost her.

## What Kager Attacks

Kager does not only attack Chara's body. He attacks the relationships that let her remain herself:

- [[characters/raiden|Raiden]] is fragmented at the story's break point.
- [[characters/javier|Javier]] is killed because Chara spared him.
- [[characters/frisk|Frisk]] is killed because her presence sustains Chara.
- [[characters/hchara|HChara]] is used as a weaponized image of Chara's worst possible endpoint.

This makes Kager personal even when he speaks in systems language.

## Chara's Refusal

Chara's answer to Kager is not purity. She becomes violent, frightening, and costly to oppose. What she refuses is his conclusion: that love is only a vulnerability and that perfection is worth more than damaged people.
