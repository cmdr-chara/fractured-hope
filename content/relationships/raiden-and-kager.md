# Raiden and Kager

[[characters/raiden|Raiden]] and [[characters/kager|Kager]] are the story's hidden philosophical opposition — hidden because Raiden is absent from most of the narrative, present only in fragments, notes, and what other people remember about him. Kager exists in person. Their relationship is the engine under the surface of the entire conflict.

## The Shared Project

[[characters/axel|Axel]] describes their relationship directly:

> _"Brothers in everything but blood."_

[[characters/kager|Kager]] elaborates in Arc 5:

> _"Raiden and I built the theory together. Perfect universe, perfect results. No more variables, no more loss. But he got distracted. He found you. Started talking about love as if it was a solution instead of a complication... love is the system itself. And systems that run on love are designed to fail."_

This is the origin of the conflict: not hatred, not rivalry, but a shared project that one of them abandoned. Kager did not simply oppose Raiden. Raiden was Kager's collaborator before he became something else.

The perfection theory is not explained in full detail by the reviewed sources. What is confirmed is that it was a joint intellectual project and that Raiden genuinely believed in it at one point. The story suggests Raiden believed in something worth leaving. It does not clarify exactly what he believed it was.

## Raiden's Departure

Raiden stopped working on the perfection theory when he found [[characters/chara|Chara]]. This is Kager's account, and no contradicting source is available. The manuscript treats Kager's narration of this fact as accurate even if his interpretation of it is not.

What Raiden built instead:

- the reset anchor for Chara
- the soul fusion research designed to give her a path to survive Kager
- the notes and failsafe devices that structured her entire post-Prologue arc
- the wasp knife message: _"Choose the pain. Choose the failure. Choose them."_

Every tool Chara uses against Kager came from Raiden. Raiden did not abandon the project to rest. He redirected it.

## Raiden's Nightmares

The manuscript confirms that Raiden had recurring nightmares in which he screamed Kager's name. The specific content of those nightmares is not detailed in reviewed sources. The existence of them suggests the relationship was not clean even before the split — that whatever Kager represented to Raiden created something that could not be put down entirely.

## Kager's Response

Kager did not let the departure stand. He fragmented Raiden's Lumina — not just killing him, but targeting the architectural layer of his identity and memory, scattering the pieces across realities so that everyone who knew Raiden would lose him proportionally to how deeply they had connected.

This is not a coincidental method. It is a specific one. Fragmenting Lumina is the most damaging thing possible in this story's metaphysics because Lumina is defined as _"the architectural drawings of identity written in light and memory and love and every small moment that made someone themselves."_

Kager destroyed the one thing Raiden had built his departure around.

## The Philosophical Opposition

The manuscript frames this as a real disagreement rather than cartoonish villainy:

- **Kager's position:** Love is an instability. Variables that introduce loss should be eliminated. A perfect universe is achievable if the variables are controlled.
- **Raiden's position:** A universe without loss is not better. Damaged, incomplete, and full of real people is worth choosing over perfect and empty.

Neither position is held naively. Raiden knew what Kager was capable of when he left the project. He built an entire architecture for Chara to survive it.

## What Remains Unresolved

> **Unresolved:** The concrete nature of Kager and Raiden's shared history before the project — whether they grew up together, met in a research context, or came from a shared institution — is not established in reviewed sources.

> **Unresolved:** Whether Raiden's nightmares indicate guilt, fear, or something else about the original relationship is not confirmed.

> **Unresolved:** The full content of the perfection theory they built together is not documented.

> **Unresolved:** Kager's emotional relationship to Raiden's departure is partially implied but not fully stated. The destruction of Raiden's Lumina reads as personal, but the manuscript does not confirm that directly.
