# Canon Policy

This wiki distinguishes between four categories of claim. Editors should be consistent about which category a given statement belongs to.

## 1. Confirmed Canon

Events, dialogue, and details that appear directly in the primary sources: `FracturedHope_Complete.md` and `Archive/flashback.txt`. These are stated as fact on wiki pages without hedging language.

_Example:_ "Chara is 25 at the story's start." This is sourced directly from the manuscript.

## 2. Inferred Relationships

Connections and causality that the text implies but does not state outright. These should be presented with light hedging or placed in clearly marked sections.

_Example:_ the identity of the second person involved in Raiden's fragmentation — Javier confirms a second actor exists but does not name them. The wiki notes the gap rather than filling it.

## 3. Metaphysical Theory

The in-universe mechanics of souls, Lumina, and timeline behavior as characters understand them. These may be accurate within the story's logic or may reflect a character's incomplete knowledge. The wiki presents them as the text presents them, not as established external truth.

_Example:_ Axel's account of Kager's abilities. This is what Axel says. Whether it is complete or accurate is not confirmed.

## 4. Thematic Reading

Interpretation of what the text is doing at a structural or thematic level. These should be clearly separated from factual content — typically in a final section labeled **Notes**, **Thematic Function**, or **Thematic Weight**, or introduced with explicit framing such as "The text suggests" or "This reads as."

_Example:_ the locket image change as a metaphor for Lumina loss. The text presents the image. The interpretation is the wiki's.

---

## Merge Artifacts

The primary manuscript contains visible merge artifacts from its drafting history. The wiki extracts canon meaning from the text. It does not preserve or reproduce drafting noise.

When two versions of the same scene appear to conflict, editors should note the tension rather than arbitrarily resolving it, unless one version is clearly superseded by later material.

---

## Uncertainty Protocol

When canon is genuinely unclear:

- state what the text confirms
- state what it leaves open
- do not fill gaps with plausible inference presented as fact
- do not hedge confirmed facts with unnecessary qualifiers

The goal is a wiki that is useful and honest — not one that performs certainty it does not have, and not one that drowns confirmed facts in excessive qualification.
