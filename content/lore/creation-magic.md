# Creation Magic

Creation magic in _Fractured Hope_ is the capacity to bring new things into being rather than only modify, weaponize, or destroy what exists. It appears in the source material as both a literal ability and a thematic throughline. Its most sustained association is with [[characters/chara|Chara]].

## The Obsession

The manuscript describes Chara's "creation-magic obsession" in the pre-story period. During Library imprisonment and the period of Lab C7's research, creation magic represents Chara's insistence on building something — on producing rather than only surviving or enduring. This obsession is not strategically useful in the immediate institutional sense; it is an act of self-assertion against a system that wants her classified, contained, and instrumentalized.

The obsession precedes and survives the trauma. It connects to the broader argument the story makes about [[characters/kager|Kager]]'s position: _"Love is just another word for 'I haven't figured out how to weaponize this yet.'"_ Kager's worldview reduces everything to weapons and variables. Chara's creation impulse resists that reduction.

## Mechanical Properties

The manuscript does not provide an exhaustive theory of creation magic's rules. What is confirmed:

- It is a distinct capacity from combat abilities — it produces new objects or configurations rather than modifying or destroying existing ones.
- It is associated with [[lore/determination|Determination]] but not reducible to it — the will to create is a use of Determination's persistence-oriented properties directed outward rather than inward.
- The [[lore/model-500-blueprint|Model 500 blueprint]] is a product of this capacity.

> **Unresolved:** Whether creation magic operates according to specific rules, costs, or limits — beyond the emotional/thematic ones — is not detailed in reviewed sources.

## Narrative Role

Creation magic is what separates Chara from the institutional framing that defined her as a weapon or a threat. The Red Genocide classification is about what she can do to the system. Her creation obsession is about what she can make for herself and for the people she loves. The two are in direct tension throughout the story.
