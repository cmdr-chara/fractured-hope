# Bond-Powered Transformation

Bond-powered transformation is [[characters/paloma|Paloma]]'s primary combat ability. The manuscript explicitly describes its mechanics: her power scales with the strength of her emotional bonds, and every activation trades years of her life for the force it produces.

## Quick Rules

- **Source:** emotional bonds, especially her bond with [[characters/javier|Javier]] and the chosen family.
- **Cost:** years of Paloma's life for each major use.
- **Output:** force that can reach extreme scale under extreme emotional pressure.
- **Limit:** the total number of activations and exact life-cost are not confirmed.

## Confirmed Mechanic

The source line is direct:

> _"Her power doesn't come from anger. Comes from bonds… every time she uses it, she's trading years for raw power."_

The key point is that the ability is not rage-based. It is not a simple "gets angry, gets stronger" transformation. It turns real attachment into force, then charges her lifespan for using it.

## Arc 5 Usage

Paloma uses the transformation during the Arc 5 birthday crisis, after Javier's death and during the wider collapse around [[characters/chara|Chara]], [[characters/frisk|Frisk]], and [[characters/hchara|HChara]].

The transformation is powerful enough to pressure [[characters/kager|Kager]], but it does not solve the fight. The cost remains the point: love gives Paloma force, and the story makes that force expensive.

## Thematic Function

Bond-powered transformation literalizes [[themes/chosen-family|chosen family]] as power. It is one of the story's clearest counters to Kager's claim that love is only a weakness. The counter is not sentimental: love produces force, but force extracted from love still costs something real.

## Unresolved

> **Unresolved:** The exact number of activations and the total life-cost across the story are not detailed in reviewed sources.

> **Unresolved:** The transformation's upper limit is not documented beyond the extreme Arc 5 display.
