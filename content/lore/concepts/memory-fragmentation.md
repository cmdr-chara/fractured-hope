# Memory Fragmentation

Memory fragmentation in _Fractured Hope_ is not a metaphor. It refers to the concrete disruption of [[lore/determination|Determination]]-backed memory continuity — the capacity that allows people with sufficient Determination to retain experience across reset events and endure certain forms of soul-level interference. When that continuity is externally disrupted, what remains is partial, non-linear, and unreliable.

## The Jammer Mechanism

[[locations/the-library|The Library]]'s white-room containment uses jammer-based confinement to suppress Chara's Determination functions. The primary effect is the disruption of continuous memory access. Under jammer suppression, Chara cannot fully use the Determination properties that make timeline retention and resistance to manipulation possible.

The jammer is described as a containment strategy specifically because her Determination is too high for direct interference — a Perseverance-laced dart was used for the initial abduction rather than anything that would engage Determination directly. Jammer suppression sidesteps rather than confronts that resistance.

## Effects

Fragmentation produces gaps, non-linearity, and unreliable access to specific periods of experience. The pre-story trauma period — Library imprisonment, Lab C7 experiments, early Raiden encounters — is the period most affected. The manuscript's non-linear treatment of the pre-story is partly a narrative choice and partly a reflection of this fragmentation.

Memory fragmentation also creates vulnerability. Fragmented memory means that some of what happened during the Library period cannot be confirmed from Chara's perspective alone — it requires external sources (Frisk's confession, Raiden's notes, recovered records).

## Raiden's Lumina Fragmentation

[[lore/lumina|Lumina]] fragmentation is distinct from memory fragmentation but shares the structural logic. Raiden's Lumina is fragmented during the Prologue attack. Lumina fragmentation operates on a different substrate — it affects emotional-light function rather than memory continuity — but the narrative consequences are similar: a loss of wholeness that shapes behavior going forward.

> **Unresolved:** The second actor in Raiden's Lumina fragmentation has not been identified in reviewed sources.

## Narrative Role

Memory fragmentation is one of the story's primary tools for managing trauma as information. The past is not fully accessible; it is recovered in pieces, through other people's accounts, and through objects — the locket, the notes, the lily note — that carry what memory cannot.
