# Lab C7

Lab C7 is a government facility located seven kilometers from [[characters/chara|Chara]]'s home in Brasilia. It is the site of her abduction and six-month captivity at age sixteen, documented in the flashback sequence and referenced throughout the main manuscript.

## Abduction

Chara was living alone at sixteen, in a house she had built from textbook knowledge. The building smelled of rain on hot asphalt — a scent she had engineered deliberately. It contained two beds: her own double and a twin she had placed there for a future guest.

She was taken by dart. The dart was laced with Perseverance rather than [[lore/determination|Determination]] — the manuscript implies targeted suppression, addressing her specific soul type obliquely. The abduction itself is described in a single understated line: _"a soft phut."_

## Conditions

`Archive/flashback.txt` is the authoritative source on what happened inside C7. The merged manuscript confirms the broad facts; the flashback sequence supplies the detail.

Documented methods included:

- **Integrity cord shocks** — administered every ten minutes for two-hour sessions
- **Perseverance thread extraction** — attempted soul-thread manipulation; the manuscript records this as failed
- **Sleep and food deprivation**
- **Psychological manipulation** — specifics are not enumerated in the available sources

The captivity lasted exactly six months.

## The Extinction Probability File

At some point during her detention, the facility generated a subject profile. The flashback sequence reproduces it directly:

```text
SUBJECT PROFILE: C-1 (CHARA)
SOURCE OF DATA: LIVE STRESS TEST ANALYSIS (LAB C7)
EXTINCTION PROBABILITY: 99.4%
CODENAME: THE RED GENOCIDE
```

The codename is not metaphor or editorial. It is the facility's operational designation for what they calculated Chara would cause if she survived and acted freely. [[characters/frisk|Frisk]], who was present at C7 as a government observer, confirms: _"Yes. We knew everything."_

Also found in the data room: a book written by [[characters/raiden|Raiden]] detailing genocide route mechanics. Its presence there is not explained in the flashback sequence.

## Observation and Complicity

Frisk observed Chara's sessions through a one-way mirror and recorded the data. Her later apology is explicit about this:

> _"I'm sorry. For the library. For Lab C7. I watched them hurt you through a one-way mirror, Chara. I took notes on your screaming like it was data. Told myself it was for the greater good, that you were too dangerous to let loose. I was a coward hiding behind duty."_

Chara's response does not absolve the institution. It distinguishes the person from it: _"People change. Systems don't. You're not them anymore."_

## After C7

The flashback sequence continues past Chara's release into a confrontation involving Sans and the Library — a space the manuscript describes as collapsing into Judgment Hall geometry during a lockdown. Chara accesses the data room, takes the SAVE Star, fights Sans using the star in the confrontation, and reaches Asriel. The flashback source ends mid-fight.

The main manuscript does not detail the immediate aftermath of Chara's release from C7. Its effects — the extinction probability number she has memorized, her distrust of institutional actors, and her relationship to Frisk — run through every arc.

> **Unresolved:** The manuscript does not explain why Raiden's genocide route book was in the C7 data room, or who placed it there.

> **Unresolved:** The flashback sequence ends before the Asriel confrontation resolves. The outcome is not documented in available sources.
