# Raiden

Raiden is [[characters/chara|Chara]]'s boyfriend, [[Liberado]]'s co-founder, and the lead researcher behind several of the story's most important metaphysical systems. He is removed from the story at its beginning, when the Prologue attack fragments his [[lore/lumina|Lumina]] across realities.

From that point on, Raiden exists through traces: plans, notes, devices, scent-signatures, failsafes, and the memories other characters keep losing.

## Overview

Raiden is the story's central absence. The attack against him is what breaks the world open, and Chara's attempt to keep moving without him is what drives the rest of the plot. He is not physically present for most of the narrative, but nearly every major decision in the story is shaped by what he built or what he left behind.

## Background

### Liberado and research

Raiden built Liberado with Chara in Brasilia and served as its lead researcher. His work reaches across soul fusion, reset contingencies, hidden labs, and multiverse-level structural thinking.

### Kager

His history with [[characters/kager|Kager]] is one of the story's largest unresolved relationships. Axel describes them as "brothers in everything but blood," and Raiden is said to have had nightmares in which he screamed Kager's name.

### Failsafes and hidden preparations

Before the Prologue attack, Raiden puts multiple contingencies in place:

- a note hidden under a flower at the fixed-point building
- a damaged holographic device naming Kager and [[lore/jikle|Jikle]]
- soul fusion research centered on Chara's Determination
- reset-related contingencies
- soul-stabilization notes recovered later in Arc 4

These preparations are what allow Chara to keep moving after he is gone.

## Personality

Raiden comes across as careful, compassionate, and deeply future-oriented. He plans for catastrophe, but not from coldness. His defining trait is that he sees Chara as a person instead of a threat to manage.

That quality gives the Arc 4.6 memory its weight. The first embrace matters because it is not about rescuing or controlling her. It is about recognition.

## Abilities and Expertise

Raiden is not primarily defined by direct combat. His importance lies in research and design:

- soul fusion theory
- reset and contingency planning
- hidden-lab design and temporal shielding
- artificial soul matrices research
- multiverse and timeline-focused engineering work

## Relationships

- **[[characters/chara|Chara]]** — boyfriend, co-founder, and the person whose loss structures her arc
- **[[characters/kager|Kager]]** — tied to him through a history that is emotionally and strategically damaging, though still unclear in exact form
- **[[characters/axel|Axel]]** — covert collaborator who preserves Raiden's work and memory through synthesized Determination

## Story

### Prologue

Raiden's Lumina is deliberately fragmented. This is not treated as an ordinary death. His identity scatters across realities, and the people who love him begin losing him from memory.

### Arc 1 and Arc 2

Chara recovers his notes, damaged device, and research trail. His soul-fusion work becomes the basis for the transformation that carries her into the middle arcs.

### Arc 4

Raiden's soul-stabilization notes and the first-embrace memory deepen his role from absent plot device to emotional center. Lilies, scent-signature, the locket, and remembered kindness all gather around this part of the story.

### Arc 6

His name resurfaces in the closing stretch, but the meaning of that resurfacing remains unresolved.

## Artifacts and Traces

Raiden's presence is carried by recurring objects and signs:

- the lily note
- the damaged holographic device
- the soul-stabilization notes
- the locket
- the genocide book tied to deeper containment history
- his lily-linked scent-signature
- the Model 500 blueprint
