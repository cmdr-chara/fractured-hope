# Paloma

Paloma is Javier's partner and one of the story's clearest examples of bond-powered force. Her grief in Arc 5 becomes one of the largest power escalations in _Fractured Hope_, and that escalation comes at a literal life-cost.

## Overview

Paloma moves from adversarial presence to ally, and then from ally to one of the most devastating embodiments of the story's central question: what does love let a person survive, and what does it make them spend?

## Role in the Story

Paloma first becomes important when Chara chooses not to kill Javier. From there she gradually shifts into the household's orbit, carrying both information value and emotional weight.

## Bond-Powered Transformation

Paloma's power scales with emotional bonds rather than simple anger. When she transforms, she draws on those bonds directly, and each use costs her years of life.

That is what makes her later force so frightening. It is not free power and not symbolic power. It is something her body pays for in lifespan.

## Javier

Her relationship with [[characters/javier|Javier]] gives the transformation its emotional center. Their plan to open a bakery somewhere quiet after the war is one of the most ordinary dreams in the story, which is exactly why losing it matters.

## Arc 5 and Aftermath

After Kager kills Javier, Paloma's grief becomes battlefield-scale force. She pushes back at Kager with overwhelming power, but cannot kill him outright.

She survives the immediate aftermath and later finishes the Justice revolver that Sans delivers to Chara before the Arc 6 closing battle.

## Open Questions

- How many times can Paloma draw on this bond-powered state before it kills her?
- What does her life look like after spending so much of it in combat?
- Can her power be used without the same level of self-destruction?
