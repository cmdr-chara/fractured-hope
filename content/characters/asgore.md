# Asgore

Asgore is a political and institutional figure rather than a frontline combatant in _Fractured Hope_. He represents the side of the story's violence that comes through policy, authority, and sanctioned systems instead of open criminality.

## Overview

Asgore matters because the story refuses to let Chara's suffering be blamed only on rogue actors. Some of what happened to her came through organized, Dreemurr-side institutional power, and Asgore exists somewhere inside that structure.

## Institutional Role

Asgore is tied to the state-aligned machinery that classified Chara as Subject C-1, produced the Red Genocide framing, and allowed the systems around Lab C7 and the Library to exist.

## Family Connection

Asgore and Chara share the Dreemurr name, but the exact nature of that relationship is still unclear. The story confirms the connection without fully defining whether it is biological, dynastic, political, or some mixture of those.

## Narrative Function

Asgore is important because he keeps the story's institutional violence from becoming abstract. He stands for the part of the world that processed Chara through official channels and arrived at extinction probabilities, containment systems, and "tests" rather than rescue.
